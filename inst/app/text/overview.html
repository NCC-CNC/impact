This tool is designed to evaluate new project management plans (PMP) and their impact to securing national conservation features.

Workflow

  1. Explore the achievements layer to view what is currently protected
  2. Upload a new PMP and extract national conservation themes to parcel boundaries.
  3. Compare between PMP boundaries and analyze currently protected, newly extracted and regional specific set goals to achieve 1M hectares

Framed under the lens of resilient landscapes, the aim is to build a protected reserve network that is:
C onnected - so that species habitats and processes can survive
A dequte - so that there is enough high quality habitat quality for species to persist through time
R epresentative - so that targeted habitats and important species exist on the landscape
E ffective - so that protected areas are cost-effective with objectives met


Conservation Features

Conservation features used for goal setting include: species at risk (SAR), endemic and general biodiversity.

Conservation features used for impact measurements include: key biodiversity areas (KBA), under-represented areas, climate change resilience, connectivity, direct human pressures, habitat, carbon, freshwater and access.

Conservation features have been curated to reflect the national 1km grid standard.
